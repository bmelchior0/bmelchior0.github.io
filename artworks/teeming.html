Teeming

Berenike Melchior, 2025

Video, 6:44 min and 7:27 min,
eight rocks with barnacle shells,
epoxy, glass

"Teeming" is a multimedia installation exploring the plural imaginaries captured by barnacles — those feather-footed, filter-feeding marine invertebrates most often found growing in tidal pools or on the bottom of boats. Through video and sculpture, Teeming follows barnacles, both on the former island of Vuosaari and on-line, where they are encountered as agents of infiltration, infestation, and sedimentation. In the process, these tiny creatures draw us into the time and scale of prehistoric whales, tracked through isoscaping, to shadow fleets slinking through a remilitarising Baltic and a series of AI generated animal rescue videos that are colonising the internet. In Teeming, barnacles, commonly regarded as a nuisance for their rapid marine biofouling capabilities, take the role of spies, sensors, and stowaways, and perform as economic parasites, as they drag at the hull of international shipping freighters.
