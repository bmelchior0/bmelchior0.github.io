Ferric Bodies

Berenike Melchior, 2023

3D MJF Nylon PA12 print,
9,8 x 5,5 x 6,6 cm
and Screen recording, 3:03 min

In the sciences, data is the building block of knowledge. It is recorded, transformed, translated, visualized, pulled apart, and squished together. Each step abstracts away from the original measurement process and moves iteratively towards concrete findings. In an effort to materialize the unseen, the work "Ferric Bodies", a series of objects and a video, makes tangible the characteristic X-ray absorption spectra of three iron samples (pure iron, iron(II) oxide, & iron(II,III) oxide). The translation of data, recorded during the research conducted for my bachelors thesis, into three-dimensional space projects these iron species into the material world. The three objects are scaled to lie in a human hand, allowing the fingertips to trace over the ripples in the surface. Whereas the video provides an alternative perspective, dwarfing the human in its scale, as if stood in front of a waterfall. The viewer is invited to interact with science in its material form via the vector of their body and to give in to the investigation as to what it may mean.
